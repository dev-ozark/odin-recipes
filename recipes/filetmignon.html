Filet Mignon with rich Balsamic Glaze

This filet mignon is cooked to absolute perfection, and topped with a rich balsamic glaze that leaves the entire dish melting in your mouth. Made form the finest cut of tenderloin tip, this cut of steak is a must-have.

Ingredients

  • Steaks: You will need two four ounce filet mignon steaks
  • Seasonings: A simple combination of salt and pepper is enough for this recipe. Use 1/2 teaspoon of pepper or to taste. Use enough salt to taste.
  • Balsamic vinegar: When you add the balsamic vinegar it puts the whole thing together! 1/4 cup.
  • Wine: Any dry red wine will do, such as pinot noir. 1/4 cup

Steps

  1. Get your ingredients.
  2. Lightly powder both sides of your steaks with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat a nonstick skillet to medium-high heat. Sear steaks until browned, about 1 minute per side.
  4. Reduce heat to medium-low, and pour in balsamic vinger and choice of red wine. Cover and cook for 4 minutes. Flip steaks and baste with sauce; cover and cook for another 4 minutes
  5. Remove steaks to two warmed plates. Spoon a tablespoon of glaze over each steak and serve quickly.
