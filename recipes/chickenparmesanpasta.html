Chicken Parmesan Pasta

Description

Chicken parmesan pasta, a slight twist on the classic chicken parmesan with delicious pasta and tender meat, this dish is incredible, and sure to make you love parmesan and Italian food.

Ingredients

  • Tomato or marinara sauce
  • 8 ounces spaghetti or linguine
  • 2 large boneless, skinless chicken breasts, six to eight ounces each, halved crosswise
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup dry bread crumbs
  • freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 1/4 cup extra-virgin oil
  • 1/2 cup grated part-skim mozzarella cheese
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese, plus more for serving

Steps

This recipe had the homemade tomato sauce replaced with store-bought, which cut out some steps.

  1. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti in boiling water, stirring on occasion, until tender yet firm to the bite, about 12 minutes. Drain and keep warm.
  2. To make the chicken cutlets: Lay chicken pieces between 2 sheets of plastic wrap. Use a mallet or heavy pan to beat chicken to about 1/4 inch thickness.
  3. Beat egg in a shallow dish until well mixed. Season bread crumbs with black pepper in another shallow dish.
  4. Preheat the oven broiler to high. Put a rack 4 to 5 inches from the heat source.
  5. Working with one cutlet at a time, dip chicken in beaten egg, then in bread crumbs. Set breaded cutlets on a wire rack set over a cookie sheet.
  6. Heat 1/4 cup oil in a 12-inch skillet over medium-high heat until the oil simmers
  7. Fry breaded cutlets in hot oil until golder brown on each side, about 5 minutes. Wash and ry the wire rack and return to cookie sheet; place fried cutlets on the clean wire rack.
  8. Top each cutlet with mozzarella and Parmesan cheese. Broil until cheese melts and is brown in spots, about 2 to 3 minutes.
  9. Portion pasta onto 4 plates and top each with a chicken cutlet. Spoon 2 to 3 tablespoons tomato sauce over each cutlet, then sauce pasta as desired. Serve with extra Parmesan at the table.
